Making Risk Simple


I've always been skilled at simplifying complex problems. Terms like threats, vulnerabilities, impact, likelihood, and risk prioritization can feel intimidating.

How I explain these concepts to non-technical stakeholders:
  • What could go wrong?
  • Where could it go wrong?
  • How bad would it be?
  • How likely is it?
  • Is it worth fixing right now?

Portfolio

Flagship Project:
Risk Acceptance for a Security Gap

A GRC case study evaluating a high-risk access control gap in a $2B payment processing environment. The project walks through the security gap, business context, risk assessment, compensating controls, decision options, and formal risk acceptance documentation.

What it shows:
• Risk-based decision making
• Business tradeoff analysis
• SOC 2 and PCI DSS awareness
• Clear stakeholder communication
• Practical remediation planning

Risk simplified:
A legacy payment system used a shared database administrator account, had broad password exposure, lacked database-level logging, and could not be fully replaced for 12–18 months.

Recommendation:
Accept the risk temporarily with immediate improvements, including password rotation, reduced access, and a future remediation commitment.
